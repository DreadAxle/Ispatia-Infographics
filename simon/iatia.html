The Faces of God: A Cross-Tradition View

Different supernatural factions perceive the Creator through the lens of their own history and cosmology, creating a fractured mosaic of the divine.

The One / Prime

As seen by: Mages (Celestial Chorus)

The transcendent, syncretic source of all faith and the "Song of Creation." An abstract, perfect principle.

Jehovah / The Maker

As seen by: Vampires, Demons

A History of Creation & Ruin

Fiat Lux: The First Act

From a state of absolute nothingness, the Creator's will brings forth the universe. This act establishes the fundamental framework of reality‚Äîthe "perfect balance between Is and Is Not."

The Elohim: Divine Architects

The celestial host is created not for worship, but for work. Seven Houses of angels are tasked with shaping the cosmos, each embodying a fundamental law of the new reality.

The Sin of Caine: The First Curse

After murdering his brother, Caine is cursed directly by the Creator. This act establishes a pattern of bestowing immense supernatural power at the cost of a terrible, enduring damnation.

The War in Heaven

A rebellion born of love for humanity, led by Lucifer. A third of the heavenly host defies the Creator to grant mortals self-awareness. The response is incandescent wrath, a thousand-year war, and the casting of the Fallen into the Abyss.

The Great Withdrawal

Following the war, the Creator's presence vanishes. Heaven's gates are sealed and the divine song falls silent. The universe is left orphaned, beginning its slow decay into a World of Darkness.

The Great Schism: Anatomy of a Rebellion

The War in Heaven was not a conflict of good versus evil, but a tragic clash between divine law and forbidden love for humanity, splitting the celestial host in two.

The Loyalists

Core Motivation:

The Rebels (Fallen)

Core Motivation:

To defy a silent Creator in order to grant humanity the gift of self-awareness and save them from a foreseen doom.
