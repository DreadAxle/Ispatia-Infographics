A Visual Guide to the Embodiment of the Marvel Multiverse

Who is Eternity?

Eternity is not a god or a being that lives within the universe; it is the universe. As a nigh-omnipotent abstract entity, it represents the sum total of all life, time, and existence within a given iteration of the Marvel Multiverse. Its history is the history of creation itself, a cyclical story of death and rebirth across cosmic ages. It is currently the personification of the 8th Cosmos.

Cosmic Power Profile

Eternity's abilities are emergent properties of its nature as reality itself. While nearly absolute, its power is not without specific, conceptual limitations, particularly when acting through its finite universal avatars (M-Bodies).

Major Existential Threats

Eternity's defeats serve as a narrative barometer for the greatest threats in the cosmos. These conflicts challenge the very principles of existence and are often perpetrated by beings wielding external or reality-defining power.

The Cosmic Hierarchy

Eternity, while immensely powerful, is not the supreme authority. It exists within a well-defined cosmic power structure, subordinate to the ultimate forces of law and creation while holding dominion over the conceptual fabric of reality.

The One-Above-All

The supreme creator of the Omniverse. Its power is absolute and unquestionable.

The Living Tribunal

The ultimate arbiter of cosmic law, maintaining balance across the multiverse. Eternity must defer to its judgment.

Eternity & The Cosmic Abstracts

Includes Eternity, Infinity, Death, and Oblivion. They are the embodiments of the fundamental principles of existence.

Galactus & The Celestials

Fundamental forces of nature and cosmic architects who operate within the framework established by the abstracts.

Forms of Manifestation

The distinction between Eternity's true form and its avatars is crucial. Its defeats are almost always inflicted upon its finite projections, not its true, multiversal self.

Multi-Eternity (True Form)

The sum total of all realities. This is the true, nigh-invulnerable entity that embodies the entire multiverse. It rarely interacts directly with lower planes of existence.

M-Body (Universal Avatar)

A finite, universal-level projection used to interact with beings. This is the form most often seen and challenged by heroes and villains. Its power is limited to the universe it inhabits.

The Children of Eternity

Eternity is a generative force, capable of spawning new abstract entities that personify specific concepts within its being. These "children" are not biological but are extensions of reality's conceptual landscape.

The Cycle of Creation

Eternity is the current, but not the first, embodiment of reality. The multiverse evolves through cycles, with each "Cosmos" ending to give birth to a new one with different fundamental principles. This process was initiated by a rebellion against the static perfection of the First Firmament.

1st Cosmos: The First Firmament

A single, static universe. Its creator nature led to a civil war with its creations, the Celestials, shattering it and birthing the multiverse.

2nd - 6th Cosmos: The Age of Evolution

A series of multiverses that introduced foundational concepts: infinite possibility (2nd), duality (3rd), archetypes (4th), magic (5th), and science (6th).

7th Cosmos: The First Eternity

The reality most are familiar with, born from the ashes of the 6th. This universe was ultimately destroyed by the extra-dimensional Beyonders.

8th Cosmos: The Current Eternity

Painstakingly restored after the Beyonders' destruction. This is the current state of the Marvel Multiverse, weakened at its rebirth but now fully established.

The Paradox of Power: Weaknesses & Limitations

For a being of near-infinite power, Eternity is paradoxically defined by its limits. These vulnerabilities are not flaws, but fundamental rules that provide stakes for cosmic stories and reinforce the intricate balance of the universe.

The Avatar's Burden

Eternity's defeats are almost always inflicted upon its finite "M-Body" avatars. These universal projections possess only a fraction of its true power, allowing for narratively significant threats without destroying the entire multiverse.

Conceptual Chains

It is bound by cosmic law and must defer to the Living Tribunal. It also cannot fight its direct opposite (the void/Chaos King), as to do so would be to fight an aspect of its own definition.

Overwhelming Force

Forces that operate on a universal scale (like the Infinity Gauntlet) or originate from outside its reality (like the Beyonders) can overwhelm and even destroy its M-Bodies and true self.

Internal Conflicts

Threats can arise from within. Its own creator (First Firmament) has imprisoned it, and its "son" (Entropy) once destroyed it. Its own fragmentation can also cause reality to destabilize.
