The Scarlet King

An Ontological Threat Analysis

The entity designated the Scarlet King (Khahrahk) represents a metaphysical, multiversal threat of the highest order. He is not merely a physical being but a fundamental principle of tyrannical rage, a conceptual parasite that has retroactively inserted itself into the history of all creation. His objective is absolute: the dissolution of all existence to end the suffering he perceives as its inherent state.

Singular, Nihilistic Goal

The Genesis Paradox: A Synthesis of Being

The King's origin is a self-perpetuating causal loop, making him an acausal foe. He is both a primordial deity from before time and a modern idea born of societal tension. This is his greatest power: upon manifesting as a concept, he possessed the ability to rewrite the past, creating his own ancient mythology.

This dual nature means he is an eternal constant—an invasive narrative that underpins countless realities. He exists *because* of the modern world he seeks to destroy, and simultaneously, he has *always* existed. To fight him is to fight an idea that can change the history of the conflict while it is being fought.

The King's nature is a composite of multiple symbiotic origins, each contributing to his overall terrifying persona.

A Taxonomy of Power

The Scarlet King's power is a perfect hierarchy of tyranny. His abilities flow from the highest level of abstract, conceptual manipulation down to raw, universe-shattering physical force. This structure is mirrored in the oppressive, hierarchical societies he inspires, such as the Daevite Empire.

  • Metaphysical: Retroactively alters history and narrative; exists beyond cause and effect.
  • Thaumaturgical: Master of blood magic; manipulates minds and memories across dimensions.
  • Physical: Manifests as continent-sized avatars capable of ending worlds.

Conceptual Antithesis

The King's greatest weakness is the one he created: the rebellion of his seventh daughter, A'habbat, who birthed heroes instead of monsters. This culminates in SCP-999, a being of pure positivity. The King's power is derived from pain, rage, and despair. SCP-999 is a conceptual weapon that can "unmake" him by negating these fundamental principles with joy, love, and euphoria.

Failing Containment

While the core entity is conceptual, its physical avatars can be bound. The most powerful known avatar, SCP-2317, is imprisoned by seven chains. Six have already broken. Its escape, which will trigger an XK-Class End-of-the-World Scenario, is considered inevitable by the O5 Council, projected to occur within 30 years.
